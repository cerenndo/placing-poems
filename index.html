Placing Poems.

Placing Poems is an online, interactive map for people to write about new and imagined journeys away from the stifling reality of the pandemic. For much of the past year, we have been restricted by contours, parameters, real or imagined. Placing Poems is your opportunity to write about these journeys and place them on a public, interactive map. From visiting a new bakery to sailing to Gola Island, you can write about any journey, no matter how grand or small. Your contribution will be one of many poetic journeys, creating a poetic cartography of anticipated adventures for everyone to enjoy.

Roan Ellis-O’Neill (he/him)is a poet and writer from Belfast. He currently works for Armchair & Rocket, a creative company that loves a good story. His academic and creative work can be found in Hold the Door Open: A Commemorative Anthology from the Ireland Chair of Poetry and Interdisciplinary Literary Studies. He coordinates and manages this project. Get in touch @Roneill2!

Ceren Dolma (she/her) is a geographer and a cartographer based in Dresden, Germany. She is a recipient of an Erasmus Mundus scholarship to complete a MSc in Cartography and is currently in her final semester. She is responsible for the technical aspects of the project, including the web development and maintenance. Find her on Twitter @ceren_do!
